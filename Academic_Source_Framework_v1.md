## 1. ACADEMIC SOURCE TAXONOMY

### 1.1 Five-Tier Source Hierarchy

| Tier | Label | Description | Weight in Citation |
|------|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **T1** | **Foundational Theory** | Seminal works, canonical theories, Nobel/Prize-winning research | 40% |
| **T2** | **Empirical Research** | Peer-reviewed meta-analyses, longitudinal studies, RCTs | 30% |
| **T3** | **Applied Frameworks** | Domain-specific models validated in practice | 20% |
| **T4** | **Case Studies** | Fortune 500 / unicorn documented transformations | 7% |
| **T5** | **Practitioner Guides** | McKinsey/HBR/BCG reports, white papers | 3% |

### 1.2 Source Quality Criteria

| Criterion | T1 | T2 | T3 | T4 | T5 |
|-----------|----|----|----|----|----|
| Peer-reviewed | Required | Required | Preferred | Optional | N/A |
| Citation count | 1000+ | 100+ | 50+ | N/A | N/A |
| Journal H-index | Q1 (top 25%) | Q1–Q2 | Q2+ | N/A | N/A |
| Author authority | Professor/Dean | PhD/Researcher | Consultant/Academic | Executive | Practitioner |
| Recency | Timeless + 5yr | 10yr | 7yr | 5yr | 3yr |

## 6. CITATION FORMAT STANDARD

### 6.1 APA 7th Edition (Primary)

**Book:**
Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of work: Capital letter also for subtitle* (2nd ed.). Publisher.

**Journal Article:**
Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of article. *Title of Periodical, volume*(issue), page range. https://doi.org/xx.xxx/yyyy

**Case Study / Report:**
Organization Name. (Year). *Title of report* (Report No. 123). Publisher. URL

**Practitioner Article:**
Author, A. A. (Year, Month). Title of article. *Name of Publication*. URL

### 6.2 Harvard Style (Secondary)

**Book:**
Author, A.A. (Year) *Title of Work*. Place: Publisher.

**Journal Article:**
Author, A.A. and Author, B.B. (Year) 'Title of article', *Title of Periodical*, Volume(issue), pp. page range.

## 9. APPENDICES

### Appendix A: Glossary of Academic Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Dynamic Capability** | The firm's ability to integrate, build, and reconfigure internal and external competences to address rapidly changing environments (Teece, Pisano & Shuen, 1997) |
| **Strategic Foresight** | The ability to create and maintain a high-quality, coherent and functional forward view, and to use the insights arising in useful organizational ways (Slaughter, 1995) |
| **Organizational Unlearning** | The process by which firms eliminate old logics and behaviors to make room for new ones (Hedberg, 1981; Nystrom & Starbuck, 1984) |
| **Absorptive Capacity** | The ability of a firm to recognize the value of new, external information, assimilate it, and apply it to commercial ends (Cohen & Levinthal, 1990) |
| **Core Competence** | The collective learning in the organization, especially how to coordinate diverse production skills and integrate multiple streams of technology (Prahalad & Hamel, 1990) |
| **Sensemaking** | The process of creating situational awareness and understanding in situations of high complexity or uncertainty (Weick, 1995) |
| **Exploration vs. Exploitation** | The tension between searching for new possibilities and using existing certainties (March, 1991) |
| **Intuitive Logics** | The dominant scenario planning methodology involving inductive reasoning from key driving forces to coherent narrative futures (Wack, 1985; Schwartz, 1991) |
| **Causal Layered Analysis** | A futures method that explores issues at four levels: litany, social causes, discourse/worldview, and myth/metaphor (Inayatullah, 2004) |
| **Transitional Object** | A concept from psychoanalysis applied to foresight: scenarios as objects that help organizations transition from present to future (de Geus, 1988) |
